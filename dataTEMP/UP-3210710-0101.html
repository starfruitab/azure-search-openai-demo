About the Introduction Chapter

This chapter contains basic information about this manual.

Safety Precautions

Before starting any work on the equipment, it is compulsory to read the Safety Precautions chapter in any of the existing manuals of the equipment.

Safety Messages Description

A safety message is always accompanied by a safety alert symbol and a signal word.The safety alert symbol is used to alert about potential personal injury hazards. To avoid hazards, obey all safety messages that follow this symbol.The following safety alert symbols and signal words are used in this manual to inform the user of hazards.
None
Danger indicates an imminently hazardous situation which, if not avoided, will result in death or serious injury.
None
Warning indicates a potentially hazardous situation which, if not avoided, could result in death or serious injury.
None
Caution indicates a potentially hazardous situation which, if not avoided, may result in minor or moderate injury. It may also be used to alert against unsafe practices.
None
Caution without the safety alert symbol indicates a potentially hazardous situation which, if not avoided, may result in property damage.

Information About this Manual

Tetra Pak recommends reading all delivered manuals carefully. Make sure that this manual is available to the personnel responsible for installing the rebuilding kit onto the machine.The Update Pages is used for updating the machine’s existing delivered manuals.

How to Use the Update Pages

The Update Pages chapters contain pages used to update the manuals.Use the content of the Update Pages chapters to update the machine manuals. The Rebuilding Instructions chapter of the Rebuilding Manual describes how to update the machine manuals with the content of the Update Pages.

Layout and Numbering

For information about page layout, consult the Introduction chapter of the machine’s existing Maintenance Manual.The Update Pages chapters use the chapter designation UP to identify the update pages in the existing machine manual.
  • the Chapter designation
  • the Consecutive page number within the Update Pages chapter
  • the Total number of pages in the Update Pages chapter

  2. Consecutive page number
  3. Total number of pages

Transfer Unit - Change Bearing Unit, Drive Unit, Geared Motor

Equipment StatusPower must be turn OFF.
Special EquipmentAssembly tool TP No. 1551475-0100
Drive mandrel TP No. 2888177
Drive mandrel TP No. 2864304
ConsumablesLubricant with anti-seizing properties for temperatures above 300°C, 100 g
    1. Roll out the LFU, see Procedure 216.1-5.1 LFU - Move to Service Position .
    2. Burn hazard.Allow the transfer unit to cool down before starting any work on it.The transfer unit and its connections can be hot.
      Hazardous voltage.The main power supply disconnector must be switched off and secured with a lock before starting any work on the electrical equipment. Will shock, burn or cause death.
      Disconnect the fuse from the Motor according to EM.
    3. Disconnect the motor cable from the Motor.
      1. Motor
    1. Remove the Connection box.
    2. Remove the flow meter unit fastening screws and the Flow meter unit.
    3. Remove the four transfer unit connection box fastening Screw.
    4. Remove the Dosing unit and plug the hole.
      1. Connection box
      2. Flow meter unit
      3. Screw
      4. Dosing unit

Prevent the transfer unit screw from falling down.

    1. Put a Plank/beam or similar on the frame and fasten the strap around the Bearing unit and the Plank/beam.
    2. Fasten a strap around the Beam and the transfer unit.
      1. Plank/beam
      2. Bearing unit
      3. Beam

  1. Assembly tool

    1. Remove the four transfer unit fastening Screw.
    2. Remove the two transfer unit fastening Screw.
    3. Remove the two transfer unit fastening Screw.
      1. Screw
      2. Screw
      3. Screw

Split the transfer unit screw from the bearing unit (only for bearing unit and drive unit)

      Note: Only valid for the bearing unit and drive unit. See next page for removal of the geared motor
    1. Remove the six transfer unit fastening Screw.
    2. Remove the cover and fit the Drive mandrel TP No. 2888177.
    3. Turn the drive mandrel while removing the Transfer unit screw from the bearing unit.
    4. Roll the transfer unit out.Note: Be careful with the thermocouples when rolling the transfer unit out.
    5. Remove the drive mandrel TP No. 2888177 (14).
      1. Screw
      2. Drive mandrel TP No. 2888177
      3. Transfer unit screw

Remove the bearing unit (only for bearing unit and geared motor)

      Note: Only valid for the bearing unit and geared motor.
    1. Remove the eight bearing unit fastening Screw.
    2. Fit the Drive mandrel TP No. 2864304.
    3. Turn the drive mandrel (17) and remove the Bearing unit.
      1. Screw
      2. Drive mandrel TP No. 2864304
      3. Bearing unit

Install (bearing unit)

    1. Apply grease with anti seeze properties on the new Bearing unit shaft.
    2. Assemble the new bearing unit.
    3. Apply grease with anti seeze properties on the Transfer unit screw shaft.Note: Check that the wedges in the new bearing unit and the transfer unit screw shaft are aligned. If not, remove the Fan cover on the transfer unit and turn the fan until the wedges are aligned.
    4. Assemble in the reverse order.
      Examples of lubricant trade names and manufacturer
      Never Seez regular grade, Bostik
      Assembly Paste (NSF H1), article# 8046 or 8045), CRC
      Metal Free Paste, article# 3050, CRC
      Food Grade Anti-Seize Lubricant, article# 8014, Loctite or Henkel
      1. Bearing unit shaft
      2. Transfer unit screw shaft
      3. Fan cover

Install (drive unit)

    1. Apply grease with anti seeze properties on the transfer unit screw shaft.Note: Check that the wedges in the new bearing unit and the transfer unit screw shaft are aligned. If not, remove the Fan cover on the transfer unit and turn the fan until the wedges are aligned.
    2. Assemble in the reverse order.
